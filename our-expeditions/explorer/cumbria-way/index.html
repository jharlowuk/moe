Expedition Details


Total length: 110km
Distance per day: 20km
Difficulty: Easy


Group size: 10
Trippers: 2
Duration: 1 week


Typical age: 18-21
Recommended experience: None
Typical cost: £500

 

 

A stunning trip across Cumbria that condenses every amazing part of a Trails expedition into a one week package. With views loved by poets and artists alike in addition to well defined walking trails, The Cumbria Way makes for a truly excellent introduction to British thru-hiking!

 

Highlights include:

  • The view from the top of High Peak
  • Following the Great Langdale Valley with a view of Skiddaw and Blencathra
  • A sunrise vigil at the start of Day 5
  • Finally making it and enjoying the last sunset from Ulverston

 

Walking the Cumbria Way is an exhilarating journey through an enticing mix of Cumbria's natural splendour and intriguing heritage. It is a walk of contrasting landscapes and changing character, including many peaceful parts of the wonderful Lake District. Its completion will give you a real sense of achievement.

Both in historic Carlisle and Ulverston there is much to appreciate, with plenty of other tempting distractions and diversions once you are underway. There is the challenge of crossing some high and more exposed ground but, overall, the trail follows low level terrain through a series of valleys.

We start from Carlisle, moving southbound towards Ulverston. This means we can help everyone learn the skills of thru-hiking at the right pace so you're into expedition life by the time you're passing through the most beautiful sections of the trail.

On this expedition, participants generally carry 3-4 days of food at a time, resupplying at Keswick. Like other Explorer expeditions, this makes it perfect for people with less lightweight packs, and ensures an easier walking experience. Accommodation is generally in established campgrounds, but depending on the group composition and speed, trippers will sometimes opt to wild camp in order to open up addition possibilities.

A Typical Day on Trail

 


Morning

 

The group wakes up at an agreed time and has breakfast together. Generally any cooking (or more importantly, coffee brewing) is done on a rotating basis by members of the group. Once breakfast is done, the team breaks camp and gets ready to go. It can take a couple of days to get used to this routine, but by the time trail is over you'll want to keep your fresh air and mountain coffee over your alarm clock at home!


The Trail Part

 

Now the group needs to walk the distance it planned on doing for the day. You'll typically move for an hour at a time, taking regular breaks in-between for water, a rest, or just to enjoy the nice view you came across. As part of the day, you might be asked to do some navigation, help with making lunch, or you might just make conversation with your friends. This part can be tiring, but you have your whole group (and two trippers) for support each day.


Evening

 

Once you arrive at your destination, we'll set up camp and make an evening meal together. You generally have a few hours of downtime every evening to play games or just get to know your group members. Now's also the time that the trippers will let you know the plan for the next day, and discuss with the group how everyones doing.

Layover Days and Vigils

 

Layover days

Typically layover days used to rest after a difficult section of trail, or are chosen by the group in order to take part in a local attraction without disrupting their travel plans. Why you take a layover day depends on the individual group, but everyone agrees that they're really fun!

 

Vigils

These are extended periods of self reflection that you undertake mid-way through your expedition. Trippers will guide you to a remote location and give you an opportunity to spend (at times) days in total, undistracted isolation. Many people feel their vigil is the highlight of a trip with us, using the time to think about their life, write poetry, create art, or even just be truly alone for the first time in years.
 

On an Explorer expedition, we generally include 1 layover day.
