Our process

Thank you for considering Trails! While we have very few requirements for our application process, please read the information provided below before sending anything our way.


Send what you want

We are excited to see whatever you think sells your experience best. Get creative, but remember that we need a solid idea of your experience and skillset, in addition to your personality.
 

Our best advice

Remember that we are foremost a community of outdoor leaders, so double check that you fit in with our social values. If you're accepted, we also hope that you'll be an active member of our community for some time. Qualifications can be important, but we care much more about your experience of life, trail, and your behaviour.
 

Keep your expectations low

Regrettably, we have a very limited number of trips each year, so rarely have spots to fill. We want to be upfront with that and hope you understand.
 


 


 

Tripper Vacancies

The lifeblood of our community. Trippership means taking total autonomy over a trip from prospecting all the way through to the end of your trip.


Job Description

At Trails, you are the author of a trip, which comes with ups and downs. If you encounter problems, we will be on hand to support and help you, but you must be ready to spend the length of your journey acting as an independent team.

Most of our trippers start by taking out Explorer or Pathfinder trips in their first year. On every trip you take with us, you will work intimately with a co-tripper and coordinator to plan, prepare, and deliver the journey. As such, we look for people who are used to sharing their leadership, great at communicating, and know how to properly 'split the load'.

We are particularly interested in hearing from BAME and non-cismale applicants.

Again, thank you for considering Trails. I can't wait to hear from you.

John Harlow, Director
john@ontrails.uk

If successful, this work will be offered on a freelance (trip to trip, daily rate) or seasonal basis (a fixed term block, weekly rate). Allocations are made on a basis of skillset and experience. You will be expected to attend a staff training week.
 

Required

- 21+ years old
- 2+ years experience working directly with young people in a similar capacity
- Outdoor First Aid (16+ hrs) or equivalent
- Possess a clean driving record
- Successfully pass criminal background and national sex offender checks
- Ability to sustain high-level of activity for 25 continuous days
- Commitment to the full duration of rostered Trails programs
 

Desirable

- Extensive and well rounded outdoor, travel, and volunteer experience.
- Wilderness First Responder (60+ hour)
- Experience in group facilitation and teaching
 

Compensation

- Competitive daily rates of pay, based on experience and qualifications
- Flexible, fair workplace invested in your professional development
