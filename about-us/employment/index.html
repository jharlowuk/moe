Our process

We are committed to fair hiring practices. As such, please familiarise yourself with our policies before proceeding with any application.

Status notification

Once submitted, your application will be reviewed and assessed by our team. We endeavour to respond to all candidates in regards to continuing the selection process, or declining to proceed with the existing application. Please be aware that applications are reviewed in batches, which may be some time after your initial submission.
 

Equal opportunities

We are an equal opportunities employer and do not use personal detail to review applications. On that basis, we ask that you do not include any personal or identifying information in your application.

Please endeavour to not reference the following:

  • Name
  • Address
  • Date of birth/age
  • Gender
  • Ethnic origin
  • Marital status
  • Religion
  • Disability
  • Sexual orientation

    •  

    Reasonable adjustment

    If you feel you require reasonable adjustments in the course of your selection process, please note them in your application email. These are removed from your application before being sent for reviewal.
     


     


     

    Field Vacancies

    Group Leaders

    Job Description

    Group Leaders work directly in the field leading groups of young people on outdoor education, service, and overlanding programs. In this role, you will be responsible for leading basic activities, group management, program logistics, and delivering on our clients educational expectations. Whilst on program, you will need to maintain a high level of energy, remain in line with our risk standards, and competently respond to incidents according to MOE protocols. We offer work on a seasonal basis, although additional work may be offered year round.

    Staff Development blocks starting year round. Based in Concord, NH.
     

    Required

    - 21+ years old
    - 2+ years experience working directly with young people in a similar capacity
    - Wilderness First Responder (60+ hrs) or equivalent
    - Possess a clean driving record
    - Successfully pass criminal background and national sex offender checks
    - Ability to sustain high-level of activity for 25 days
    - Commitment to the full duration of rostered MOE programs
     

    Desirable

    - Applicable NGB's
    - Qualifications and experience in group facilitation and teaching
    - Extensive and well rounded outdoor, travel, and volunteer experience.
     

    Compensation

    - Competitive daily rates of pay, based on experience and qualifications
    - Flexible, fair workplace invested in your professional development
    - Access to professional discounts on outdoor products
     

    How to Apply

    Applications will include:
    - Cover Letter and Resume
    - Logbook of experience
    - Applicable First Aid and NGB certificates
    - Two current contactable professional references

    Please submit these details, or direct questions regarding this posting to hr@moe.us.org
