Keeping You Safe


We take the physical and mental health of everyone on our trips incredibly seriously, which is why your safety is always our overriding responsibility.

Looking after trippers

Our supportive, inclusive community

First and foremost, Trails is a community dedicated to adventurous spirit. That means being supportive of one another, especially when times get tough.

 

Trail is a safe space

People from all walks of life come to the outdoors for the feeling of being on the inside, looking out. In the spirit of this, we do everything we can to make each member of the expedition team feel valued and included. This is a principle belief that we look for in trippers, and a heavy focus on every trip we take.
 

Zero tolerance for hate

We don't accept hate in any form, and participants who flout our community guidelines are asked to leave the trip.
 

Non-negotiables that make sense

We don't allow participants to carry any knives (we will provide these for cooking) or illegal substances. Groups can enjoy alcohol on trail as long as it is done respectfully and within our community guidelines, however we ask folks not to keep any in their backpacks.
 


About Us: Our Values

Our trips

Leaders you can trust with your life

Because our trippers are the most important link in our safety chain, we make choosing and training them our top priority.

 

A wealth of experience

All of our trippers are amazing people, and each one brings a unique skillset, extensive experience, and a love of tripping to the table. Additionally, we agonise over matching trippers to each trail they lead, so you know when you come on a journey with us that you're getting the best of the best.
 

Two trippers on every trail

We know from experience how vital this is. It makes supporting our participants twice as easy, nearly halves their (at times heavy) workload, and massively aids our leaders decision making in remote situations. It's why we guarantee two leaders on every trip we run.
 

Proper staff training

We make sure that our leaders are intimately familiar with our support systems, standards, and their co-tripper before we let them take a trip out.

Tested support structures

We know that thru-hiking can be unpredictable, so we have a range of external support that every tripper can make use of at a moments notice.

 

Support that never sleeps

Every trip gets a coordinator who is available 24/7 throughout the trail and is in daily communication with every tripper. Whenever a group needs guidance or assistance, we're there to offer a helping hand.
 

Coordinators who are ready to help

Our trip coordinators are not only experienced at managing groups from afar, but also running them as trippers. This level of knowledge means they're equipped to provide assistance which is appropriate, practical, and swift ranging from logistical support to tried and tested assistance on the ground.
 

Industry leading standards

All of our program activities adhere to a set of operating standards which match or exceed industry expectations. We carefully select, inspect, and monitor our venues prior and during the trip. If something does go wrong, our people and procedures are a safe pair of hands.

FAQ's: On trail

We look after the trippers too

Trippers can't look after you if they're exhausted themselves, which is why we're serious about making sure they're as happy as you are.

 

We don't run trips back to back

Trips with us are as exhausting for trippers as they are for participants, so we give them plenty of time to rest and relax between trips that they take.
 

We frequently check in with each other

By touching base with every tripper, we're able to better look out for one another and can spot onsetting exhaustion before it becomes a problem.
 

We don't second guess

Because we have such a good relationship with such experienced leaders, we never second guess a judgement call that your tripper makes.
