Ethical Responsibility

Our outdoor journeys are invitations for people to join a community built around particular social values.

 

Our groups are traveling communities, and intimately organised around a set of social values which are liberal, communitarian, and altruistic.

 

Specifically, these are:

  • personal scale - direct interpersonal interactions not mediated by technology (writing, telephone, fax, etc.) and against systematisation.
  • personal responsibility - people taking responsibility for the consequences of their actions, and delegating real responsibility to each other.
  • intimacy - people living in close quarters, sharing space, food, clothing, schedules, goals, joys, disappointments, their hopes, and fears.
  • tribalism - the group taking precedence over the individual.
  • service - dedication to a higher cause and looking out for the good of others.
  • egalitarianism - all members of the expedition are of equal value.
  • open-mindedness - about issues, each other, and one's rights and responsibilities.
  • humility - both in the face of challenges and before other people.
  • personal respect - the basic ground rule for interaction.
  • minimum impact on the natural world - consuming just as much as is needed to get by, thinking about the impact of one's actions on strangers.
  • simplicity - in particular non-materialism.
  • humour - responding to adversity in ways that shine light.
  • telling it like it is - the refreshing straightforward honesty of a wilderness trip.
  • trust - relationships mediated by trust as opposed to written agreements, contracts, etc.
  • meeting and managing danger - living in awareness of daily risk and allowing that to affect one's outlook, e.g., gratitude.
  • integrity - when actions, individual or collective, honour the values above attention to the maintenance of the community itself.
     


FAQ's: A Warm Welcome

Keeping you safe: Community

We share these values by bringing people into meaningful contact with them.

 

We believe that experiential learning means providing real experiences in environments which help participants to improve their nature. Our trips are designed on principles which we feel will promote interaction with our values.

 

These include:

  • remoteness - leaving normal society behind focuses group members on the world and people in front of them, and allow a clearer understanding of the cause and effect of personal actions.
  • difficult - our trips are planned to be challenging enough to expose people to real hardship, providing participants with ownership over the trip's outcome.
  • extended length - participants are given an appropriate amount of time to build the honest interpersonal relationships necessary to create a community.
  • real - while our values and goals are consistent, the results are not.
     

Our role is to promote our social values and community through challenging outdoor expeditions.

FAQ's: Enrolling



We believe that being a community means upholding our own standards.

 

Practicing what we preach means considering our environmental and social impact both administratively, and in the field.

 

Wilderness conservation

In the field, we are committed to minimising our environmental impact by following the seven "Leave No Trace" principles. By teaching and adhering to these standards, we aim to inspire a new generation of conservationists.
 

Social responsibility

We support fair employment practices, provide enough time between programs to adequately recover, and maintain a positive, inclusive work culture. We take our overriding duty to protecting physical and mental health as seriously for our staff as our participants.
 

Environmental and local impact

We are constantly working to understand, plan, and manage our use of resources to be fair, efficient, and practical. On our programs we support the communities we work with by purposefully using local infrastructure and business.
