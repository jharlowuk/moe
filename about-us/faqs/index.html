A warm welcome to our community

 

Dear friend,

First of all, thank you for considering us! We are so excited to welcome you and many other like-minded people to our out-tripping expeditions this year.

Make no mistake, attending one of our programs is a significant challenge. It will mean getting muddy, making new friends, and getting up early to see the sunrise. The flip side is that you get to get muddy, make new friends, and enjoy plenty of beautiful views.

Our aim is simple: when you are 60, we want you to look back and still be astounded by what you accomplished this Summer. More importantly, we want you to feel a meaningful connection to the people and places in your trip.

We do this with values which we don't shy away from. With real, difficult experiences which will push you far outside your comfort zone. By doing things which you and your team-mates can be proud of.

We hope that through the answers on this page and by reading about us elsewhere on this site, you'll find our approach appealing. Our firm belief is that these attributes make extraordinary life experiences, which certain people find irresistible.

We hope you'll join us.

John Harlow

Director, Trails Outdoor Group

Enrolling on a trip

 

What is the right trip for me?

Unless you are already an experienced thru-hiker, we recommend picking an Explorer or Pathfinder journey. Explorers are challenging journeys, but ideal for those who are new to expedition life or seeking a more moderate challenge. Pathfinders are more difficult trails, and therefore we recommend having prior experience (such as DofE or similar) before signing on.
 

Are your trips split gender & what are your age restrictions?

All our journeys are 18+ and co-ed. Typically, participants are between 18-25 and the gender split can vary form trip to trip. We encourage you to talk to us if group composition is a concern when considering an expedition. These restrictions do not apply to CES trips.
 

The application process

We like to get to know you a little bit before we sign you on so we know you're the right fit for your trip. Anticipate a few emails and a phone call before we officially register you. Sometimes we might recommend another trail to you based on that information, but obviously this is just a suggestion!
 

Your fees

We have a variety of payment plans and options to suit everyone. All fees are due prior to the trip start date. If you have any questions about payment before you sign on, we hope we can be of service.
 

Pre-trip planning

Once you're registered, we'll put you in touch with your trippers who will be able to advise you on how to prepare for your journey, answer your questions, and may chose to put you in touch with other participants. It's a great opportunity to get a head start on the trip!

Preparing for your trip

 

How can I mentally and physically prepare for trail?

Great question! Thru-hiking can be a daunting prospect, but always remember that you are joining into a supportive community when you enroll. Before your trip, get to know your trippers and fellow participants by group chat for help and support in preparing. If you are concerned about your level of physical fitness, get in touch with your trippers for practically minded, sensitive advice. Remember, all of our trips are difficult but achievable!
 

What do my expedition fees cover?

Typically, your trip cost covers all your food, accommodation, leaders (trippers), and transport from and back to Sheffield. You are likely to spend a little more than these fees on equipment (and we can rent you gear if you need it) and we recommend brining around £150 of spending money on your trip.
 

What equipment is provided?

We provide most equipment, including tent, mats, and group kit such as tarps, stoves, and utensils. You will need to bring some equipment of your own such as a sleeping bag and backpack, but this will be made clear when you sign up. Participants are always welcome to bring their own kit on their thru-hikes.
 

Food allergies, dietary requirements, and medicals

We can cater for (almost) everyone, but we will need all your food, allergy, and dietary requirements at least four weeks prior to your program starting. During this time, we will also attain your medical information. It's only a couple of forms, and takes less than an hour to complete!

"On trail"

 

How do trips start?

Expedition groups meet on Day 0 in Sheffield, and spend the day briefing and preparing for the trip. This is an opportunity to get to know your group, get systems down, and solve any last minute problems. On Day 1 we run a sunrise ceremony to say goodbye, before sending you to the trailhead with your group. From there, the journey begins.
 

What if the weather is poor?

We run our trips regardless of the weather. If it is exceptionally poor, we sometimes run a layover day.
 

What happens in the event of an incident?

Part of the fun of a Trails journey is problem solving, but sometimes you might come an issue that you need external help to solve. In these cases, we have a strong support network, extensive incident response plans, and adequate insurance for all our programs. Rest assured, you're never far away from help.

After your trip

 

What happens at the end of the trip?

Once you reach the end of your trail, we'll pick you up and bring you back to Sheffield where you'll enjoy a return meal, ceremony, and stay the night in a hotel. In the morning, your expedition group will meet one final time to debrief before finally parting ways and returning to your friends, family, and the human world.
 

What happens after the trip?

Most groups keep in touch for some time after the end of their trips, and we facilitate this in a few ways. After completing a trip with us, you become a Trails Alumni, which not only makes keeping in touch easier, but also grants you some additional benefits. Post-trip, we'll also send you opportunities to share photos from your journey.
