East Coast

 

White Mountains, USA

Covering a quarter of the state of New Hampshire, the White Mountains offer a combination of untouched alpine wilderness, excellent trail infrastructure, and historic appeal which make it a dream for outdoor education programs.


Walking

Take your young people along the Appalachian Trail to the Presidential Range, where they can conquer New Englands highest peak. With more 4000 footers than you could manage in several programs, and countless waterfalls, gorges, and notches to see along the way, your group won't be lacking for things to see and do on their trip.


Canoeing

Further south in the Lakes Region, the stunning Lake Winnipesaukee and Lake Squam provide a perfect setting for single and multi-day canoe journey's. Within walking proximity of many mountains, they allow for clients to add a day of canoeing to a walking journey.


Climbing

The nearby Rumney offers sport climbing and abseiling activities, with convenient basecampjng opportunities. Further afield, Whitehorse Ledge offers some more daunting, exposed traditional climbing for small, adventurous groups.

Arcadia National Park, Maine

A rich landscape of ocean, wildlife, and mountain, Arcadia is a highly diverse national park which can cater to a wide range of clients.


Walking

While juniors will love the seaside strolls and ocean views, older young people will love summiting its tougher peaks for their stunning vistas. In short, everyone can find a beautiful trail at Arcadia to suit their experience and age.

Climbing

Sea cliff climbing at Arcadia is an unforgettable experience, which provides an exhilarating vertical perspective of the ocean for young people. Abseiling towards your challenge forms a great mental commitment, which stays with participants long after conquering the climb.

Boston, Massachusetts

On a MOE urban program, students see cities through a new perspective. In Boston, students explore its historical roots, see a thriving city in action, and provide help to communities in need.


Culture

Both one of America's oldest cities, and home to many of its firsts, there is plenty to learn of its past and present. Follow the freedom trail to discover its extraordinary history yourself. Visit museum, galleries, and memorials, and have groups learn how to safely navigate the city.


Service

During our urban explorer programs, young people can learn experientially how other people live, the impact cities have on the natural world, and how difficult life can be for those in difficulty. Working with one of our partners, or on an internal project, groups contribute their time to make the city a better place.

Great Smokey Mountains, Tennessee

A UNESCO World Heritage Site for good reason, the affectionately named Smokies are famous both for world-class sunsets, and challenging mountain trails.


Walking

Young people should prepare for a difficult uphill battle should they take on the many 6000 foot peaks, but with plenty of options elsewhere, the Smokies have a walk for everyone. Offering many camping options, and manageable temperatures year-round, this is a surprisingly versatile and adaptable walking venue.


Climbing

Taking a short journey west from the mountains, you can quickly find excellent sport climbing and abseiling opportunities for groups at Clear Creek, and convenient nearby camping.

North America

 

Hawaii

People often travel to Hawaii for it's postcard perfect beaches and world-class surfing, but learning its fascinating Polynesian history, and helping to tackle the increasing threat to it's native flora and fauna will surely make you want to stay much longer.


Service

Whether you're travelling deep into the heart of an ahupua'a to dig out invasive species and replant native flora, or staying on the shorelines to clean beaches and raise public awareness of turtle nesting sites, our service projects aim to get your young people in touch with local people, and local issues.


Culture

Attend a luau, learn about Polynesian culture, and visit the Pearl Harbour Memorial. Hawaiian's are both proud of their diverse past, and eager to share what makes their home unique.


Adventure

Surf, snorkel, and SCUBA dive in Hawaii's stunning blue ocean, and share the space with its incredible aquatic wildlife. Then, take inland to zipline above farmland, and hike deep into its mountains.

Costa Rica

Covered in rugged rainforest, nestled in Central America, and offering everything from volcano to unforgettable beaches, Costa Rica is an idea destination for animal lovers, adventure seekers, and culture lovers.


Service

Costa Rica is world-renowned for its dedication to environmental protection and we support a number of local initiatives working to protect the natural environment and promote sustainability. On its coastline, volunteer at a turtle conservation centre, or take your team inland and work at a sustainable permaculture farm aiming to reforest former pasture land.


Culture

At Costa Rican farms, discover how coffee and sugar is grown in the cloud forest of Monteverde, before travelling to the coast to discover the ins and outs of permaculture. Eat typical Costa Rican dishes, learn Spanish through immersion, and stay in places that the locals live in.


Adventure

With a thriving adventure tourism industry, Costa Rica can be enjoyed anywhere from trilling zip-lines above its rainforest canopy, to whitewater thrill rides below it. Take a night hike through the with a local guide, or ride electric bikes across the beach, there's something here for everyone.

Europe

 

Snowdonia, Wales

Dubbed "the adventure capital of the UK", Snowdonia is a perfect venue for difficult and highly adaptable outdoor programs. With fantastic walking and climbing across it's area, this is one of the most popular outdoors locations in the UK.


Walking

We typically stray away from the honeypot massifs, sending clients to unfamiliar, challenging areas which help train their navigation skills, and offer a better environmental connection, all for an improved wilderness experience. With lots of opportunities for valley and wild camping, this is an excellent venue for trekking journey programs.


Climbing

See Snowdonia from an entirely different perspective as you climb or scramble up its mountains, or spend a relaxing day at a single pitch venue, taking on harder climbs, and encouraging your group. With opportunities for both traditional and sport climbing across the area, you can include climbing at any point in a journey or expedition.

Lake District, England

Among Britains best known National Parks, the Lakes have inspired many a writer, including its own Wainwright. It isn't hard to understand why; from Helvellyn to Skiddaw, its rugged landscape combined with picturesque namesakes make a sight as enjoyable in its valleys as its summits.


Walking

Boasting Englands highest peak, steep terrain, and challenging weather, the Lakes are a fantastic venue for groups looking to for a challenging hike. It's also an excellent place to learn complex outdoor skills including micro-navigation, wild camping, and scrambling.


Canoeing

Unsurprisingly, the Lakes offer great opportunities to learn and practice canoeing skills in large, open water. Flanked on all sides by mountains, paddling across these waters is a stunning and unique experience.

Highlands, Scotland

A stunning venue which is often overlooked in favour of its southern counterparts, the Highlands offer a robust, truly wilderness adventure that can challenge novice and experienced outdoors people alike.


Walking

Offering some of the largest undeveloped land in the UK, the Highlands are a perfect venue for expedition programs to explore its endless horizons. From its peaks, you'll be treated to an unforgettable view of its mountains and the sea. As you go between them, test your navigation skills, learn about the areas geology, and enjoy the experience of extended wild camping.


Canoeing

The Highlands are our best UK based multi-day canoe venue, with stunning lochs that will take you days to reach the end of and come back. Integrate a canoe section into an existing program, or make your entire journey revolve around traversing a beautiful, non-tidal, mountainside lake.
